A bit of a short and random post but, what's the deal with modern programming tools treating me like a baby all the time. I mean I don't mind having batteries included but when simple features are built, without thinking of common use cases, it can create some unexpected problems. What I'm talking about is the classic divide by that only shows up when someone else writes a new unit test or a filter in a query which sneakily includes null values. What irks me is not those errors themselves but, the fact that quite often the functions they are the results of something unexpected happening with a tool or dependency; in the olden days I could at-least blame myself for writing a poorly abstracted model or function. Now I can only point fingers at the people spoon feeding me the formula, aka software vendors or open source volunteers (which I'd like to be 100% clear that I appreciate the work they do.)
